# Stock Thesis Review Framework

Source: user's personal stock thesis review framework. Reference for SPEC-023 — the in-app thesis form sections, scenarios model, and check-lists mirror this doc 1:1.

A reusable framework for analyzing a stock the same way we reviewed Netflix: start from your theory, fact-check it, strengthen it, challenge it, and turn it into a practical watchlist and add/hold/sell framework.

This is for research and decision support, not personalized financial advice.

## 2. Analysis Flow

### Step 1: Restate the thesis

Turn the investment idea into clear claims.

Example:

| Thesis point | Type | Needs proof? |
|---|---|---|
| Asia is underpenetrated | Core growth driver | Yes |
| Games could improve engagement | Optional upside | Yes |
| Valuation is reasonable | Valuation claim | Yes |
| Company has pricing power | Core thesis | Yes |

The key is to separate **core thesis drivers** from **optional upside**.

Core drivers must justify the valuation. Optional drivers can improve upside, but should not carry the whole investment case.

---

## 3. Source Hierarchy

Use better sources for stronger claims.

| Source type | Best use | Trust level |
|---|---|---:|
| Annual report / 10-K / 10-Q | financial facts, risks, official strategy | high |
| Investor letter / earnings call | management guidance and roadmap | high |
| Official macro data | inflation, rates, consumer trends | high |
| Reputable financial data | price, market cap, multiples, estimates | medium-high |
| Industry research | market share, TAM, category growth | medium |
| News | recent catalysts and events | medium |
| Reddit/forums | sentiment, bull/bear arguments, crowd narratives | low-medium |

Rule: Reddit and forums are useful for discovering arguments, but not enough for factual proof.

---

## 4. Fundamental Review

Use this table every quarter.

| Area | What to check | What good looks like | Red flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue growth | latest quarter, TTM, guidance | stable or accelerating | sharp slowdown |
| Margins | gross, operating, net | stable or expanding | compression without clear reason |
| Free cash flow | reported and normalized | strong and repeatable | boosted mainly by one-offs |
| Balance sheet | net debt, cash, maturities | flexible and manageable | rising leverage |
| Segment/region growth | product/geography trends | growth in important markets | core region stagnation |
| Guidance | management outlook | raised or stable | cut guidance |
| Capital allocation | buybacks, M&A, reinvestment | disciplined | overpaying or desperate moves |

Important question:

> Did the business get weaker, or did only the stock price fall?

---

## 5. Market Position and Growth Runway

Ask:

1. What market is the company really competing in?
2. Is it gaining or losing share?
3. What is the true competition?
4. What does the company dominate today?
5. Is that dominance durable?
6. What new markets or product lines can expand the opportunity?
7. Are those new areas proven, or still optional?

Use this split:

### Core growth drivers

- Customer/user growth
- Pricing power
- Revenue per user/customer
- International expansion
- Margin expansion
- Free cash flow growth
- Buybacks or capital return

### Optional upside drivers

- New products
- New content formats
- Games
- Podcasts
- AI/product improvements
- Live events
- Partnerships
- Adjacent markets

## 6. Valuation Review

Use more than one metric.

| Metric | Best for | Watch out for |
|---|---|---|
| P/E | profitable companies | can look cheap if earnings are temporarily high |
| Forward P/E | expected earnings | depends on analyst estimates |
| EV/EBITDA | mature operating businesses | can ignore capex/content economics |
| Price/free cash flow | cash generation | distorted by timing and one-offs |
| EV/sales | high-growth companies | dangerous if margins disappoint |
| DCF | long-term cash flow | very sensitive to assumptions |
| Reverse DCF | what price implies | depends on chosen assumptions |

Core valuation questions:

- What revenue growth is the current price assuming?
- What margin is the current price assuming?
- What multiple must the market keep paying?
- What happens if the company performs well but the multiple compresses?
- Is the stock cheap, fair, or simply high quality?

---

## 7. Scenario Analysis

Avoid one price target. Use ranges.

| Scenario | Business assumptions | Valuation assumptions | What it means |
|---|---|---|---|
| Worst case | growth slows, margins compress, competition increases | multiple compresses hard | stock can fall 30-50%+ |
| Bear case | company still okay, but expectations fall | lower but not disaster multiple | stock may be attractive only if thesis intact |
| Base case | management roughly delivers | normal multiple | expected return depends on starting valuation |
| Better case | strong but realistic execution | premium but not extreme multiple | meaningful upside possible |
| Moonshot | major long-term compounding | premium multiple + large earnings base | possible, but needs time and execution |

Worst-case questions:

- What would make the stock fall 50%?
- Would that be a broken thesis or just a valuation reset?
- What data would prove the bear case is becoming real?
- Would I still want to own it if the stock dropped but fundamentals weakened?

Better-case questions:

- What can revenue become in 3-5 years?
- What can margins become?
- What can EPS or free cash flow become?
- What multiple would be reasonable then?
- Is the upside worth the risk?

## 8. Add/Hold/Sell Checklist

### Green light to add

Most of these should be true:

- Price fell mostly because of market weakness or valuation compression.
- Revenue growth remains close to guidance.
- Margins are stable or expanding.
- Free cash flow remains strong after adjusting for one-offs.
- Key growth drivers are still working.
- Competitive position is stable or improving.
- Management is executing the roadmap.
- Valuation is more attractive than before.
- Position size remains within your risk limit.

### Yellow light: slow down

Use smaller tranches or wait when:

- Growth slows but does not break the thesis.
- Margins are slightly weaker.
- Guidance is mixed.
- The stock is cheaper but still not clearly cheap.
- New initiatives are promising but not proven.
- Macro pressure is rising.

### Red light: stop and re-underwrite

Do not average down mechanically when:

- Management cuts guidance sharply.
- The most important growth driver stalls.
- Margins deteriorate without a temporary explanation.
- Free cash flow weakens structurally.
- Competitors take durable share.
- The company overpays for growth.
- Balance sheet risk rises.
- The original thesis is no longer true.

### Trim or sell triggers

Consider trimming or exiting when:

- Valuation becomes far ahead of realistic growth.
- The thesis has played out.
- Better risk/reward opportunities exist.
- Position size becomes too large.
- Fundamentals weaken over multiple quarters.
- Management credibility declines.

## 10. Copy-Paste Prompt

Use this prompt when you want the analysis done.

```markdown
Act as my long-term stock thesis review partner.

I will give you a ticker, my thesis, my position, and what I am thinking about doing. Your job is to:

1. Restate my thesis clearly.
2. Fact-check it using the latest company reports, filings, market data, macro data, and credible industry sources.
3. Separate core thesis drivers from optional upside.
4. Analyze fundamentals: revenue, margins, free cash flow, balance sheet, guidance, and quality of earnings.
5. Analyze market position, growth runway, moat, competition, and roadmap.
6. Analyze valuation using multiple methods and explain what the current price assumes.
7. Build worst-case, base-case, and better-case scenarios.
8. Create a green/yellow/red checklist for adding, holding, trimming, or stopping an averaging-down plan.
9. Tell me what I may be missing, what I may be overvaluing, and what I should monitor next quarter.

Use clear tables when helpful. Be honest and challenge my assumptions. Do not give me personal financial advice or tell me exactly what to buy or sell. Give me a decision framework.

Here is my stock thesis:
[PASTE THESIS]
```
